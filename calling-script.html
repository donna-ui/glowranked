Charter Listing Call Script

Use this for outbound calls to IE med spas. The goal: qualify them, not sell to them. Verification is free. The paid upgrade is their decision after they see value.

Phase 1 — Open
Pattern interrupt. Don't sound like a sales call.
💡
Call at 10am–11am or 2pm–3pm. Never Friday afternoon. Ask for the owner or practice manager — not the front desk.
If gatekeeper answers: "Hi, I need to leave a quick message for the owner — it's about their GlowRanked listing. Do you know if they handle that themselves?"
"Hi, is this [Name / Owner's Name]? … Great. Hey, this is [Your Name] from GlowRanked — I'll keep this really short.

We're building a verified med spa directory for the Inland Empire — think of it as the trusted, AI-verified alternative to Yelp — and [Spa Name] came up on our shortlist for the [City] launch. I'm actually calling because we can only verify one or two spas per neighborhood, and your spot is still open. I wanted to reach out to you before I called the next spa on our list." Pause here. Let them respond. Do not fill the silence.
Phase 2 — Qualify
Flip the script. You're choosing them.
"Before I explain how this works — can I ask you one thing? How are new clients finding you right now? Like outside of word of mouth and Instagram — do you have a way to capture people who are actively searching for Botox or laser in [City] and just… don't know you exist yet?" Listen fully. This tells you their pain. Repeat it back later.
If they say "Google" or "SEO": "That's good. The challenge with Google search results is you're competing with everyone who buys ads. GlowRanked only shows verified providers — no ads, no pay-to-play. So when someone searches and sees your listing, it means something."

If they say "word of mouth": "That's how the best places grow — the issue is the ceiling. Someone searches for a med spa at 11pm, they're not asking a friend, they're on their phone. We want to be where they land."
Phase 3 — The Reveal
They're already on the site. This is a warm call, not cold.
"So here's where we are — we've actually already created [Spa Name]'s listing on GlowRanked as part of our charter launch. You're live right now, tagged as 'Invited to Verify.' We only shortlist spas that pass our initial screening — license check, reputation check, safety record — so the fact that you're on there means you already cleared the first round.

The listing is visible, but it's in a pending state. We can only upgrade it to Verified once we've completed the full 12-point check — and that's what I'm calling about." If they want to check: glowranked.com/find — they'll see their name there.
Phase 4 — The Takeaway
Scarcity is real. Don't over-sell it. Just state it.
"Now I want to be upfront with you — we're doing this very deliberately. We verify one, maybe two spas per zip code. The whole reason clients trust the directory is because they know we're not listing everyone. If we verified every spa, it would mean nothing.

So I do have [a competing spa / the next spot / another spa in your area] that I'm following up with this week. I'm not trying to pressure you — I just want to give you first shot." Do not name specific competitors unless you know one. "Another spa in the Chino Hills area" is enough.
Phase 5 — Remove the Risk
Verification is free. Say it clearly. Say it once.
"The verification itself is completely free. It takes about ten minutes on your end — you fill out a short form, we do the rest. We pull your licenses, authenticate your reviews, run the safety screening. No credit card, no commitment.

The paid tiers are optional — those are for spas that want priority placement, featured spots, and the marketing add-ons. But the base verification? That's on us. We want the directory to actually be useful, and that means having real spas in it."
Phase 6 — Close
Get the email. Send the link while they're on the phone.
"I can send you the verification link right now — it's a short form, takes five minutes. Does [their email from Google/website] still work for you?"

[Send the link: glowranked.com/claim]

"I just sent it. Subject line is 'Your GlowRanked charter listing — [Spa Name].' Once you fill that out, we do the verification on our end and your badge goes live. Usually takes 24–48 hours." If they don't have email handy: "I'll text it to you — do you prefer that?" Or offer to call back. Don't let the call end without a commitment.
Objection Handlers
Stay calm. Don't defend. Keep it short.
"What's the catch? What are you selling?"
"Honestly — nothing on this call. The verification is free, the listing stays free. We make money when verified spas decide to upgrade for premium placement — better position in search, featured spots, that kind of thing. But that's their choice after they're verified and can see the value. Today is literally just about getting you verified."
"How many clients will this send me?"
"I'm not going to make you a promise on that — we're in launch mode in the IE and I want to be straight with you. What I can say is: every verified listing gets the AI badge and ranks higher when clients search the directory. The clients who use GlowRanked are specifically looking for a spa they can trust — they're higher-intent than a random Google click. But how many? That depends on your treatments and your reviews. We just make sure you're findable to the right people."
"I need to think about it / talk to my partner"
"Totally fair — I'm not going to push you. Only thing I'd say is: the Chino Hills verification window is open right now, and I do have another spa on my list this week. I'll hold your spot until [end of this week / specific date]. Let me send you the link so you at least have it — you don't have to decide anything today, just have it in front of you."

Then send the link and set a follow-up: "I'll check back in on [day] — sound good?"
"How much does it cost?"
"Verification is free — no card needed. If after you're verified you want to look at the paid listings, they start at $197 a month for priority placement. But that's completely separate from today's call. Today is just getting your badge sorted."
"I've never heard of GlowRanked. Who are you?"
"We're new — that's actually the point. We're the directory that's building the right way from day one, not retrofitting trust into an old platform. Every listing is verified before it goes live. We're launching in the IE specifically because it's underserved — you don't have a directory that actually vets providers here. Being a charter member means you're listed before the general public even knows we exist — that's first-mover advantage."
"We already get enough clients / we're busy"
"That's the best problem to have — seriously. If you're already full, this still matters: verified listings protect you from losing clients to unverified competitors who undercut on price. Clients who find you through GlowRanked tend to be higher-value — they're not just price shopping. And the listing is free, so there's no downside to having it. Busy practices protect their reputation — this is part of that."
"Can you just email me the info?"
"Of course — I'll send it right now. [Send the link.] Just so you know what to look for: subject line will be 'Your GlowRanked charter listing.' The form takes about five minutes. Is there a better day to follow up — would [Wednesday] work?"
